Data flow across the L2C orchestration stack

How a natural-language request becomes action — and how data and telemetry flow back. Paired with a per-layer technology options matrix across AWS, Azure, GCP, and open source.

01 · Data flow

One request, six layers, two directions

A user's intent (downward arrows) traverses the stack to reach systems of record. Results, state, and telemetry (upward arrows) flow back to produce a summarized answer — with observability captured at every hop.

Layer
Data that moves — downward request · upward response · events

Down

Lakehouse (historical) · federation (live) · canonical model · vector

Up

Platform services
Identity · observability · policy · secrets — cross-cutting, always-on
In
Every hop calls policy (OPA) for authorization, secrets for credentials, identity for token exchange
Out
Spans, metrics, audit records, eval scores — all correlated by trace_id back to the original user turn
Audit
Immutable log (agent decision · tool call · approval · outcome) retained 7 years for compliance
